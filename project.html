About: Deep Learning for Low Resource NLP

This course aims to introduce the computational linguistics perspective on NLP, as understanding a language is required prior to application design. After the introduction of NLP, therefore, deep learning has introduced and explained how languages with or without limited resources can be handled. There are numerous applications for language processing, such as machine translation, opinion mining, speech recognition, speech synthesis, and reasoning. This course will introduce students to various language-based challenges and their solutions by applying computational linguistics, natural language processing, and deep learning.


Prerequisites

Basic Natural Language Processing

Course Content

Introduction to Natural Language Processing: Language and Types of Language, Phonetics, Phonology, Morphology, Syntax, Semantics, Pragmatics, Discourse Analysis, WordNet, Tokens, Types, BPE, tf-idf, CBOW, Gram-based model, Similarities, Conditional Generation, Document Level Analysis

Introduction to Deep Learning: Discriminative and Generative models, Models with Latent Random Variables, Teacher forcing, Encoder-decoder models, Attention Mechanisms-Local, Global, Soft, Chunk, Self and Multi-head, ELMo, BERT, BART, GPT, Advanced Search, Adversarial Methods

Handling Low Resource: Data Augmentations, Fine-tuning, Transfer Learning, Meta Learning, Zero-Shot Learning, Multi-task, Multi-domain and Multi-lingual Learning, Prompting, Bias and Fairness

Applications of NLP: Opinion Mining, Sequence Labelling, Machine Translation- Inter-lingua, Statistical and Neural, Speech Recognition and Synthesis, Knowledge Base and Reasoning
